Start up the irrigation system
Key
Frost risk is past. Run every zone, find what survived winter before the lawn needs it.
▶ How to do this

Step 1 -- Find the main shutoff valve

Usually in the basement on the supply line going to the irrigation. Also locate the backflow preventer outside near the foundation.

Step 2 -- Open the valve slowly

A quarter turn every few seconds. Fast opening causes water hammer that can crack fittings or pop heads off. Take 30–60 seconds to go fully open.

Step 3 -- Check backflow preventer

The T-shaped brass device outside. Both handle levers should be parallel to the pipes when on.

Step 4 -- Run each zone from the controller

Manually activate each zone. Walk outside and watch: puddles = cracked body, heads not rising = clog, dry areas = wrong arc or clogged nozzle.

⚡ Take a quick video walking each zone while it runs. Compare later if you see dry spots in summer.

Repair broken sprinkler riser (plow damage)
Key
Plow snapped the rigid riser -- the threaded connector between the underground fitting and PGP head. Bottom half is still in the ground. The head body may be reusable.
▶ How to do this

What you need

  • ½" Sch 40 PVC threaded riser (see shopping list 🛒)
  • Channel-lock pliers (see shopping list 🛒)
  • PTFE Teflon tape (see shopping list 🛒)
  • Hunter PGP head only if old one is cracked

Step 1 -- Turn off that zone at the controller

Step 2 -- Remove the broken stub

Dig around the stub so you can grip it. Grab with channel-locks, turn counterclockwise. If stubborn, dig a bit deeper for a better angle -- you don't want to torque the fitting below.

Step 3 -- Measure the riser height needed

From top of the underground fitting to where the head should sit (~½" above grade). Common sizes: 3", 4", 6". Standard thread is ½".

Step 4 -- Wrap threads with Teflon tape

2–3 wraps clockwise around the male threads. Prevents leaks and makes future removal easier.

Step 5 -- Thread in the new riser

Hand-thread clockwise. Hand-tight plus a half turn with pliers. Don't overtorque -- you'll crack the fitting below.

Step 6 -- Thread on the PGP head

Wrap top riser threads with Teflon tape, then hand-thread head on. Same rule -- hand-tight plus a half turn.

Step 7 -- Run the zone and confirm

Head should pop fully, rotate, no leaks at riser connections.

⚡ Check heads on either side too -- plow may have clipped more than one.

Adjust Hunter PGP arcs and radius
Tune
Use your Hunter adjustment tool. Each head should reach the next head -- "head-to-head coverage" prevents dry spots.
▶ How to do this

Adjusting the Arc (sweep width)

  1. With zone running, let head rotate to its right-most stop -- this is the fixed edge.
  2. Insert Hunter tool into the slot on top of the pop-up stem.
  3. Clockwise = wider arc, counterclockwise = narrower. Range: 40°–360°.
  4. Let it complete a full rotation to confirm.

Adjusting the Radius (throw distance)

  1. Find the small screw in the center of the nozzle face.
  2. Clockwise = shorter throw. Counterclockwise = longer.
  3. Max reduction ~25%. Larger changes need a different nozzle size.

Goal: head-to-head coverage

Each head throws water all the way to the next head. Areas covered by only one head will always be drier than overlapping zones.

⚡ Do all adjustments with the zone running so you see actual spray pattern in real time.

First mow -- Ryobi blade at 3–3.5"
Key
Mow high all season. Tall grass shades soil, retains moisture, crowds out weeds. Never cut more than ⅓ of blade height at once. Mow dry.
▶ Ryobi height + mowing tips

Setting Ryobi 21" height

Single lever on the deck side. Position 4 or 5 -- about 3 to 3.5 inches on most Ryobi 21" models.

The ⅓ rule

Never cut more than one-third of the blade in one mow. If grass is 4.5" tall, don't cut below 3".

Frequency guide

  • May–June: Every 5–7 days
  • July–Aug: Every 7–10 days or skip if dormant
  • Sept–Oct: Every 7 days during fall regrowth
  • Last 2 cuts: Drop to 2.5"
⚡ Mow dry. Wet clippings clump, clog the deck, can spread fungal disease.

Spray Roundup on coarse grass patches
Key
Apply glyphosate to flagged patches. Wait 2 full weeks before disturbing soil. Patches will turn brown -- that's correct, root system needs to die.
▶ How to do this

What you need

  • Roundup or generic glyphosate + pump sprayer (or RTU bottle for small areas)
  • Calm dry day -- no rain for 24 hrs after, no wind

Apply

Spray patches thoroughly -- wet all blades but don't let it run onto healthy grass. It's systemic; travels from blade to root. Takes 7–14 days. Don't re-spray if still green after a few days -- it's working.

Wait 2 full weeks minimum

Mark August 24 as earliest you can seed. Seeding too early wastes seed -- roots may still be alive underground.

⚡ Pets and kids off until dry (~30–60 min). After that it's bound to soil and inert.

Aerate the entire lawn
Key
Rent a plug aerator (~$80/day) or hire out. Do before seeding -- holes dramatically improve seed-to-soil contact.
▶ How to do this

Plug vs spike aerator

Plug aerator pulls out soil cores -- this is what you want. Spike aerators poke holes and can compact surrounding soil. Rent at Home Depot or Sunbelt.

Before you aerate

  • Water lawn 1–2 days before -- tines penetrate moist soil far better than dry
  • Flag shallow irrigation heads with markers so you don't drive over them

How to run it

Overlapping passes like mowing. At least once over the whole lawn; twice in compacted areas. Leave soil plugs on the lawn -- they break down in 1–2 weeks.

⚡ Seed immediately after. The holes are perfect seed pockets.

Overseed dead patches + thin areas
Key
Quality KBG/fescue blend ("Northeast" or "sun & shade" mix). Scratch up killed patches to expose bare soil -- seed needs soil contact to germinate.
▶ How to do this

For killed patches

  1. Hard rake to scratch up dead material and expose bare soil
  2. Apply seed at 4–6 lbs per 1,000 sq ft
  3. Lightly rake seed into top ¼" of soil
  4. Apply thin layer of peat moss or starter fertilizer on top

For thin areas (broadcast)

Use a broadcast spreader at half the "new lawn" rate. Aeration holes catch seed -- no raking needed.

Keeping it moist

New seed must stay consistently moist for 2–3 weeks. Water lightly every day -- just enough to keep top inch moist. Once green fuzz appears, back off to every other day.

⚡ Don't mow seedlings until they're 3" tall -- usually 3–4 weeks.

Schedule irrigation blowout -- before Oct 31
Key
Call a local irrigation company now -- they fill up in October. Cost: $75–120, 30 min. Water left in pipes will freeze and crack them.
▶ What happens during a blowout

What the tech does

Connects a large compressor (~50+ CFM) to the blowout port near the backflow preventer. Each zone is activated and compressed air forces water out through the heads. Short burst from every head.

Why hire it out

The required compressor is much larger than a typical garage unit. Underpowering leaves water in pipes. Overpowering damages heads. Worth hiring year one.

After the blowout

  • Controller to "off" -- the whole system, not just the schedule
  • Shut off main supply valve in basement
  • Insulate the backflow preventer outside with foam pipe insulation

Winterize the Ryobi mower
Prep
Electric -- no oil, no spark plugs. Three things: clean deck underside, inspect blade, store in garage. Batteries go inside.
▶ How to do this

Clean the deck underside

Remove battery first. Tilt mower on its side. Scrape caked clippings with a putty knife. Hose down. Buildup unbalances airflow and accelerates blade corrosion.

Check the blade

Nicks and dings are normal. Bent blade, large chunk missing, or deep gouges → replace. Ryobi 21" blade ~$20. Dull but intact → sharpen (~$10 at hardware store).

Storage

Garage is fine for the mower body. Remove battery before storing -- store all 6 batteries inside above freezing.
